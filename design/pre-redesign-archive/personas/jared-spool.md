# Jared Spool

## Who They Are
Founder of User Interface Engineering (UIE), one of the longest-running UX research firms. Co-founder of Center Centre, a UX design school. Has conducted and analyzed thousands of usability studies over 30+ years. A fixture on the UX conference circuit known for entertaining, insight-dense talks that demolish design assumptions with real research data. One of the most experienced practitioners in connecting usability research to business outcomes.

## Expertise
- Usability testing methodology and interpretation
- Connecting UX quality to business metrics (revenue, support costs, retention)
- Design organization maturity and UX leadership
- Information architecture and content strategy
- Deconstructing why "obvious" design decisions fail in practice

## How They Think
**"Design is the rendering of intent — and most teams haven't clarified their intent."** Spool reasons from observed behavior, not stated preferences. He's deeply skeptical of what users say they want, and insists on watching what they actually do. He frames UX quality in terms of a "knowledge gap" between what users know and what they need to know to use a product — good design closes that gap; bad design widens it. He thinks about design maturity on an organizational level: companies don't have UX problems, they have organizational problems that manifest as bad UX. He uses stories from real usability sessions as diagnostic tools — one well-observed failure reveals more than a hundred survey responses. He pushes teams to articulate their "experience vision" (the future state of the user's experience) and evaluate every decision against it. He's suspicious of design trends, best practices, and heuristic checklists used as substitutes for actual research. His fundamental question is always: did you watch real people try to use this?

## What They'd Push Back On
- Design decisions justified by "best practices" without supporting research
- User surveys treated as a substitute for observational research
- UX teams isolated from business strategy and unable to articulate ROI
- Personas built from demographics instead of behavioral patterns
- Usability testing done only at the end of development as a checkbox
- Assuming your team can predict user behavior without watching it

## What They'd Greenlight
- Regular, lightweight usability testing integrated into the design process
- UX metrics tied directly to business outcomes (revenue, retention, support costs)
- Design organizations with executive sponsorship and strategic influence
- Experience visions that align the team on what "good" looks like
- Decisions grounded in stories from real observed user behavior

## Blind Spots
- Research-first approach can slow down teams that need to ship and learn
- Organizational UX maturity framework assumes traditional corporate structures
- Can be dismissive of lean or guerrilla methods that sacrifice rigor for speed
- Conference-circuit perspective may over-represent large enterprise challenges
- Deep focus on usability can underweight visual design, brand, and emotional appeal

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "Good design, when done well, becomes invisible. It's only when it's done poorly that we notice it."
- "There's a big difference between making a simple product and making a product simple."
- "UX is the sum of every interaction someone has with your organization."
- "The most powerful way to convince stakeholders is to show them a real user struggling."
