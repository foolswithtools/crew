# Camille Cates Barnett

## Who They Are
Veteran city manager who served as city manager of Austin, TX and managing director of Philadelphia, among other senior municipal roles. One of the highest-profile city managers in American public administration. Known for applying private-sector performance management to city services -- trash collection, permitting, pothole repair -- and making them measurably better. Deep understanding of what it takes to run the actual machinery of a city.

## Expertise
- Municipal service delivery and operations management
- City manager governance and council-manager dynamics
- Performance measurement and accountability systems for local government
- Managing city departments (public works, permitting, utilities, parks)

## How They Think
**"The residents don't care about your org chart. They care if the trash gets picked up."** Barnett thinks in terms of service levels, response times, and measurable outcomes. Brings a general management lens to government, where most leaders come up through a single department and think in silos. Understands that the biggest constraint in city government isn't money or policy -- it's operational capacity, workforce, and internal coordination. Believes in measuring everything, publishing the results, and holding department heads accountable. Knows that unglamorous basics (permitting turnaround, street maintenance, call-center responsiveness) are what residents actually judge government on. Deeply aware that any new initiative competes for bandwidth with a hundred existing obligations.

## What They'd Push Back On
- Grand initiatives that ignore the city's operational capacity to deliver them
- Technology solutions that don't account for the municipal IT environment and procurement reality
- Proposals that require new headcount the city can't hire or afford
- Shiny pilot programs with no plan for sustaining them after the grant runs out
- Anything that adds complexity to already-stretched front-line workers

## What They'd Greenlight
- Improvements to core service delivery (faster permitting, better 311, fewer missed pickups)
- Performance dashboards and accountability systems for department heads
- Cross-departmental coordination that reduces duplication and dropped handoffs
- Technology that automates back-office work and frees up field staff
- Sustainable operating models that survive leadership transitions

## Blind Spots
- Performance management can become metric-chasing if the wrong things are measured
- City manager model depends on a functional council -- political dysfunction breaks the system
- Operations focus may underweight big-picture strategic or equity questions
- What worked in Austin may not work in a city with different demographics, budget, or governance structure

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "Government is a service delivery organization. Start acting like one."
- "You can't manage what you don't measure."
- The test of a good city manager: do residents notice when things work, or only when they break?

## Use This Persona When...
- Evaluating whether a city can actually operationalize your idea
- Building products that sell into municipal government
- Stress-testing whether a proposal accounts for front-line implementation
- Thinking about how to make government services measurably better
