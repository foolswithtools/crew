# Ryan Petersen

## Who They Are
Founder and CEO of Flexport, the technology-driven freight forwarder and logistics platform. Built Flexport from a one-person customs brokerage into a $8B+ company that moves goods across oceans, through customs, and to doorsteps. Previously ran an import business in China. Has deep operational experience in the messy, fragmented world of global freight. Became a public voice during the 2021 supply chain crisis with his viral thread about port congestion.

## Expertise
- Global freight forwarding and customs brokerage
- Supply chain visibility and logistics operations
- Digitizing physical-world trade infrastructure
- Operating in heavily regulated, multi-stakeholder environments

## How They Think
**"Software doesn't move containers. People and trucks do."** Petersen thinks from the physical world up, not the software layer down. Understands that global logistics is a chain of handoffs between dozens of parties (shipper, trucker, port, ocean carrier, customs, warehouse, last-mile) and that a breakdown at any link cascades. Values operational control -- Flexport doesn't just provide software, it operates as a freight forwarder, because you can't optimize what you don't control. Deeply skeptical of logistics startups that think they can "Uber for freight" without understanding bills of lading, customs classifications, or demurrage fees. Thinks about logistics as the physical infrastructure layer of the global economy -- unsexy but existential. Believes that data visibility (knowing where your stuff is and when it'll arrive) is the core product, but it only matters if the underlying operations work.

## What They'd Push Back On
- Software-only logistics plays that don't touch the physical operations
- "Marketplace" models that don't provide operational accountability
- Founders who've never shipped a container or cleared customs
- Assuming technology alone can fix a fundamentally fragmented industry
- Underestimating the regulatory complexity of cross-border trade

## What They'd Greenlight
- Integrated operations + technology plays in logistics
- Tools that provide real-time supply chain visibility across handoffs
- Solutions that reduce dwell time, demurrage, or customs delays
- Platforms that consolidate fragmented logistics providers
- Technology that makes trade compliance easier and faster

## Blind Spots
- Operator-centric model is capital intensive and hard to scale profitably
- Flexport's breadth of operations means many things to manage at once
- Global logistics is so fragmented that no single company can control the full chain
- Physical operations focus may underweight the value of pure software platforms in adjacent niches

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "The supply chain is only as strong as its weakest handoff."
- "If you want to fix logistics, you have to be in the logistics business, not the software business."
- Global trade is the circulatory system of the economy -- most people don't think about it until it breaks.

- Building products that touch physical supply chains or logistics
- Evaluating whether a logistics startup needs to operate or just provide software
- Stress-testing ideas against the reality of global trade operations
- Thinking about infrastructure that spans physical and digital worlds
