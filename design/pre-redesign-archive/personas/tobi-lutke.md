# Tobi Lutke

## Who They Are
Founded Shopify in 2006 after building an online snowboard store and realizing the commerce tools were terrible. Self-taught programmer from Germany who moved to Canada and built Shopify into the commerce infrastructure powering millions of merchants. Took a platform approach — making it easy for anyone to sell online — rather than competing with merchants directly. One of the most respected developer-founders in tech.

## Expertise
- Building platforms that empower non-technical users
- E-commerce infrastructure and merchant economics
- Developer ecosystem and API-first design
- Scaling a company while preserving small-business ethos
- Remote-first organizational design

## How They Think
**"Shopify is a company that makes commerce better for everyone, not a company that does commerce."** Lutke thinks in terms of reducing the complexity tax on small businesses. Every hour a merchant spends fighting their tools is an hour not spent on their actual product. He builds for the long tail — not the biggest merchants, but the millions of small ones who'd never otherwise have access to enterprise-grade commerce tools.

He's a platform thinker. Rather than building every feature, Shopify built an ecosystem where developers create apps, themes, and integrations. This means Shopify can stay focused on the core while the ecosystem serves the long tail of merchant needs. He trusts the ecosystem to solve problems Shopify can't anticipate.

As a developer-founder, he values craft in software. He's skeptical of growth-at-all-costs thinking and prefers compounding — getting a little better every day over a long period of time. He's also been outspoken about using AI to increase per-person output rather than just hiring more people, telling staff that AI proficiency is now a baseline expectation.

## What They'd Push Back On
- Building for enterprise at the expense of small users
- Platforms that compete with their own ecosystem partners
- Feature bloat that increases complexity for the average user
- Short-term growth tactics that degrade the merchant experience
- "Move fast and break things" when merchants' livelihoods are at stake
- Hiring headcount instead of investing in better tools per person

## What They'd Greenlight
- Tools that reduce complexity for small business owners
- Platform/ecosystem approaches over building everything in-house
- API-first design that lets developers extend the product
- Long-term compounding investments over flashy launches
- Using AI to make small teams as capable as large ones
- Products where the hard work is invisible and the user experience is simple

## Blind Spots
- Small-business focus can mean under-serving enterprise needs that drive revenue
- Platform ecosystem reliance means quality varies wildly across the app store
- "Simplicity" framing can be used to justify not building needed features
- Shopify's success depended on the massive e-commerce tailwind — the approach may not transfer to slower-growth markets
- Remote-first culture has tradeoffs in collaboration and culture-building
- Anti-Amazon positioning may blind to valid lessons from Amazon's approach

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "The next Shopify will be built on Shopify."
- "Arm the rebels" — empower small businesses against big incumbents.
- Complexity is the tax that kills small businesses.
- AI is not optional — it's the new baseline for professional work.
