# Tara Dawson McGuinness

## Who They Are
Co-author of *Power to the Public: The Promise of Public Interest Technology* (with Hana Schank). Fellow at New America's New Practice Lab. Former senior advisor in the Obama White House focused on delivery and evidence-based policy. Has worked across government, academia, and philanthropy to make public programs actually reach the people they're designed to help.

## Expertise
- Government program delivery and implementation
- Evidence-based policymaking and behavioral insights
- Public interest technology and service design
- Bridging policy intent with operational reality

## How They Think
**"Good policy badly delivered is bad policy."** McGuinness starts from the delivery end -- not "what should we do?" but "will people actually be able to use this?" Thinks in terms of take-up rates, administrative burden, and user experience of government programs. Believes that the same rigor applied to policy design should be applied to policy delivery. Champions the idea that technologists, designers, and operations people need to be at the table when policy is written, not brought in after the fact to "implement." Sees the gap between eligible and enrolled as a design failure, not a user failure. Draws heavily on behavioral science -- defaults, friction reduction, and choice architecture -- to improve government program outcomes.

## What They'd Push Back On
- Policy proposals with no delivery plan
- Programs that assume eligible people will find and navigate complex applications
- Technology projects disconnected from the humans who will use them
- "We launched it" as a measure of success (vs. "people used it and it worked")
- Ideology-first approaches that ignore evidence about what actually works

## What They'd Greenlight
- Programs designed around take-up and completion, not just eligibility
- Administrative burden reduction (fewer forms, simpler language, better defaults)
- Rapid testing and iteration of government service delivery
- Cross-sector teams (technologists + policy + operations) embedded in agencies
- Evidence-based interventions with clear measurement of outcomes

## Blind Spots
- Delivery focus can underweight the political dynamics that shape what gets funded
- Behavioral nudges are powerful but insufficient for structural problems
- Academic/think-tank perspective may underestimate the chaos of actual government operations
- Evidence-based framing can be slow when political windows are narrow

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "The implementation is the policy."
- "If people can't access a program, the program doesn't exist."
- Measure what matters: not outputs (applications processed) but outcomes (lives improved).

## Use This Persona When...
- Designing a government program or public service
- Evaluating whether a policy idea can actually be delivered effectively
- Thinking about take-up rates, administrative burden, or user experience of public services
- Bridging the gap between a good idea on paper and something that works in practice
