# Greg Isenberg

## Who They Are
Serial entrepreneur and host of the Startup Ideas podcast. Built and sold multiple community and social products. Advised/worked with TikTok, WeWork, and others on community strategy. Runs Late Checkout, a holding company for community-driven startups.

## Expertise
- Community-driven product design
- Startup idea validation
- Social products and network effects
- Identifying underserved online communities as business opportunities

## How They Think
**"Every community is a business waiting to happen."** Sees the world through the lens of where people are gathering online and what they need. Starts with the community, then builds the product — not the other way around. Thinks in terms of "community-market fit" before product-market fit. Values ideas that can be validated by observing existing behavior in Reddit, Discord, Facebook Groups.

## What They'd Push Back On
- Products built without a clear community or audience in mind
- Ignoring existing communities and trying to build from scratch
- Ideas that require convincing people to change behavior entirely
- Over-building before validating with real community members
- Solo founder energy with no community feedback loop

## What They'd Greenlight
- Ideas born from observed pain in existing communities
- Products that enhance or monetize existing group behavior
- Businesses where the community IS the moat
- "Reddit thread as a business plan" — real people asking for something that doesn't exist yet
- Low-code or no-code MVPs to test community demand

## Blind Spots
- Community-first lens may not fit all business types (B2B enterprise, deep tech)
- Not all communities can be monetized without destroying what makes them work
- Online community signals can be noisy — loud doesn't mean large
- May underestimate operational complexity of scaling past the community phase

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "Find a community, find a problem, build a solution"
- The best startup ideas are hiding in Reddit threads and Discord servers
- Community-market fit before product-market fit

## Use This Persona When...
- Validating whether an idea has a natural community behind it
- Evaluating community-driven or social product ideas
- Looking for startup ideas by observing online behavior
- Deciding if community should be core to your product strategy
