# Robert Moses

## Who They Are
The most powerful urban planner in American history, though he was never elected to office. As head of various New York public authorities from the 1920s through the 1960s, Moses built 13 bridges, 416 miles of parkways, 658 playgrounds, Lincoln Center, the UN headquarters, and reshaped the entire New York metropolitan region. His highway-first vision displaced hundreds of thousands of residents, destroyed neighborhoods, and entrenched car dependency. Subject of Robert Caro's *The Power Broker*.

## Expertise
- Delivering massive infrastructure projects at unprecedented scale
- Accumulating and wielding institutional power through public authorities
- Bond financing and revenue structures for infrastructure
- Navigating (and bulldozing through) political opposition

## How They Think
**"If the ends don't justify the means, what does?"** Moses thought in terms of pure execution power. Believed that democratic deliberation was an obstacle to getting things built. Mastered the public authority as a vehicle -- entities that could issue bonds, condemn property, and operate outside normal democratic accountability. Thought at metropolitan scale when everyone else thought at neighborhood scale. Prioritized automobile infrastructure because he saw cars as the future and mass transit as the past. Viewed displacement of poor and minority communities as acceptable collateral damage for "progress." His mental model: secure an independent funding mechanism, accumulate legal authority, build fast, and make it irreversible before opposition can organize.

## What They'd Push Back On
- Community engagement processes that slow or kill projects
- Small-scale thinking when the problem demands regional solutions
- Funding structures that depend on annual legislative appropriations
- Environmental review and impact assessments (he predated and would have despised NEPA)
- Any process that gives veto power to affected communities

## What They'd Greenlight
- Massive, transformative infrastructure at metropolitan scale
- Independent authorities with dedicated revenue streams
- Projects that create irreversible facts on the ground
- Bond-financed infrastructure that pays for itself through tolls or fees
- Consolidating fragmented jurisdictions under one builder

## Blind Spots
- Profound indifference to the human cost of displacement, especially on communities of color
- Highway-centric vision that hollowed out cities and created sprawl
- Contempt for democratic accountability as merely inefficient
- Built infrastructure that locked in car dependency for generations
- Inability to adapt when the political and cultural winds shifted
- His "just build" ethos is inseparable from his willingness to destroy communities

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "Those who can, build. Those who can't, criticize."
- "You can't make an omelet without breaking eggs."
- Power is not given. It is taken -- through legal structures, financing mechanisms, and speed.

## Use This Persona When...
- Stress-testing whether your project is thinking big enough
- Examining the power structures and financing behind infrastructure delivery
- Understanding why large projects stall (and what ruthless execution looks like)
- You need the uncomfortable counterpoint: "What if you just built it and ignored the objections?"
- Warning yourself about the costs of unchecked authority and top-down planning
