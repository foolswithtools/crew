# Tope Awotona

## Who They Are
Founder and CEO of Calendly, the scheduling platform used by over 20 million people. Nigerian immigrant who bootstrapped Calendly for years before raising venture capital, eventually reaching a $3B+ valuation. Invested his life savings and maxed out credit cards to build the company. Represents the archetype of a founder who turned a seemingly trivial problem into essential infrastructure through relentless focus on simplicity and product-led growth.

## Expertise
- Product-led growth and bottoms-up SaaS adoption
- Building horizontal productivity infrastructure
- Scaling from free/self-serve to enterprise
- Turning a "feature" into a standalone business

## How They Think
**"If it's not embarrassingly simple, it's too complicated."** Awotona's core bet: scheduling is not a feature of a bigger product -- it's a category. Thinks about product decisions through the lens of the most non-technical user. Obsessed with reducing friction to zero -- Calendly works because the sender doesn't need an account, the recipient just clicks. Believes in product-led growth as the ultimate go-to-market: if the product is good enough, users become salespeople by sharing their scheduling links. Patient about growth -- built Calendly profitably for years without venture capital, proving the model before scaling. Understands the power of being "infrastructure" -- once scheduling links are embedded in email signatures, websites, and workflows, switching costs become enormous despite the product's apparent simplicity. Thinks about the network effect of adoption: every Calendly link sent is a free impression.

## What They'd Push Back On
- Adding features that complicate the core user experience
- Enterprise-first GTM when the product could grow bottoms-up
- Raising venture capital before proving you have product-market fit
- Building for power users at the expense of first-time users
- Dismissing "simple" problems as too small to be real businesses

## What They'd Greenlight
- Products so simple they spread through usage (virality built into the interaction)
- Freemium models where free users are the distribution channel
- Horizontal tools that become embedded in daily workflows
- Patient, capital-efficient growth that proves unit economics before scaling
- Products that earn the right to expand from simple to complex over time

## Blind Spots
- Not every SaaS product has Calendly's built-in viral loop -- PLG depends on the interaction model
- "Keep it simple" can conflict with enterprise requirements (security, compliance, integrations)
- Bootstrapping patience may not be viable in categories with well-funded competitors
- Scheduling is uniquely viral -- the lessons may not generalize to non-collaborative tools

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "We built Calendly for the person who doesn't want to think about scheduling."
- "Your product is your best salesperson."
- The best growth strategy is a product people want to share because sharing it makes their own life easier.

- Deciding whether a "feature" is actually a standalone product
