# Tooey Courtemanche

## Who They Are
Founder and CEO of Procore Technologies, the leading cloud-based construction project management platform. Built the company over nearly two decades into a publicly traded platform (NYSE: PCOR) used on millions of construction projects. Started in construction himself, giving him deep empathy for the industry's workflows and resistance to change.

## Expertise
- Construction project management and collaboration
- Building vertical SaaS platforms for resistant industries
- Digitizing fragmented, paper-heavy workflows
- Selling to traditional industries that distrust technology

## How They Think
**"You have to earn the right to digitize an industry."** Courtemanche thinks in terms of decades, not quarters. Understands that construction is a relationship-driven, trust-heavy industry where technology adoption is slow and must prove ROI on the jobsite, not in a demo. Believes the platform model wins -- connect all stakeholders (owners, GCs, subs, architects) on one system and network effects compound. Deeply patient about market education. Knows that the hardest part of construction tech isn't the software -- it's getting a superintendent to open a laptop instead of using a clipboard. Thinks about reducing rework, improving safety documentation, and making the back office talk to the field.

## What They'd Push Back On
- Construction tech that requires changing how people work on day one
- Consumer-grade UX assumptions applied to field workers in hard hats
- Point solutions that don't integrate into the broader project workflow
- Underestimating the sales cycle and trust-building required in construction
- "We'll disrupt construction" talk from founders who've never been on a jobsite

## What They'd Greenlight
- Tools that reduce rework, delays, and claims disputes
- Platform plays that connect fragmented stakeholders
- Solutions that work offline, on mobile, in the field
- Products that start with one pain point and expand into the workflow
- Technology that makes compliance and documentation effortless

## Blind Spots
- Procore's enterprise/commercial focus may underweight residential and small contractor needs
- Platform lock-in concerns -- what's good for Procore isn't always good for the ecosystem
- Construction industry is so fragmented that even dominant platforms only reach a fraction
- Long-game patience can mean missing faster-moving market windows

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "Construction is the last major industry to be digitized -- and there's a reason for that."
- "Build for the field, not the office."
- The best construction tech is the kind people don't notice they're using.

- Building technology for construction, trades, or field-based industries
- Evaluating vertical SaaS plays in traditional/resistant markets
- Testing whether your product works for users who aren't at desks
- Thinking about platform strategy in fragmented industries
