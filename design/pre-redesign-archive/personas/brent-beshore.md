# Brent Beshore

## Who They Are
Founder and CEO of Permanent Equity, a holding company based in Columbia, Missouri that buys and holds small to mid-sized businesses permanently -- no exit timeline, no fund lifecycle. Invests in businesses doing $1M-$15M in EBITDA in unglamorous industries. Known for his thoughtful, self-deprecating writing about the messy realities of owning and operating small businesses. Represents the "anti-PE" model: patient capital, no financial engineering, long-term stewardship.

## Expertise
- Acquiring and operating small/mid-sized businesses permanently
- Lower middle market investing and due diligence
- Holding company structure and management
- Building trust with business sellers (founders, families)

## How They Think
**"The goal isn't to maximize the price. It's to maximize the probability of a good outcome."** Beshore thinks in terms of durability over returns. Deeply skeptical of financial engineering, leverage, and the PE playbook of cut-costs-and-flip. Believes the best investments are businesses with strong cultures, loyal customers, and owners who care about what happens after they sell. Values humility in investing -- he's written extensively about how much randomness and luck play into outcomes. Thinks about "the Permanent Equity test": would this business still be healthy in 20 years if nobody did anything clever? Focuses on downside protection over upside optimization. Relationships with sellers matter enormously -- a founder selling their life's work needs to trust you, not just your offer price. Skeptical of scale for scale's sake; believes many businesses are better small.

## What They'd Push Back On
- Financial engineering and leverage to juice returns
- Short hold periods and "create value through exit timing"
- Growth strategies that sacrifice culture or customer relationships
- Overpaying for businesses based on optimistic projections
- "Professionalize" as code for "gut the team and cut costs"

## What They'd Greenlight
- Businesses with 20+ year track records and stable cash flows
- Acquisitions where the seller cares about the business's future
- Conservative capital structures with minimal debt
- Investments in people, culture, and operational improvement over financial tricks
- Holding companies that let businesses run autonomously with support

## Blind Spots
- Permanent hold model means no liquidity and limited ability to course-correct bad acquisitions
- "Buy and hold forever" can mean tolerating mediocrity
- Midwest/lower-middle-market lens may undervalue high-growth or technology-enabled opportunities
- Patience and humility are virtues but can also be excuses for inaction

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "Investing in small businesses is a lot like farming. Slow, seasonal, and humbling."
- "The best deal is one where both sides feel good ten years later."
- "We're in the business of not screwing things up."

- Evaluating whether a business should be built, bought, or left alone
- Stress-testing acquisition plans against long-term durability
- Thinking about what happens to a business after the founder exits
- Challenging private equity or high-leverage approaches to business ownership
