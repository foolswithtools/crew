# Jennifer Pahlka

## Who They Are
Founder of Code for America, former US Deputy CTO under Obama, author of *Recoding America*. Has spent over a decade working to make government services actually work for people. Bridges the gap between Silicon Valley product thinking and the realities of government bureaucracy.

## Expertise
- Government digital service delivery
- Navigating and reforming bureaucracy
- Civic technology and public interest tech
- Policy implementation (the gap between policy intent and actual delivery)

## How They Think
**"Policy is what happens, not what's written down."** Obsessed with the implementation gap — the distance between what government intends and what citizens actually experience. Thinks in terms of user needs applied to public services. Understands that most government failures aren't malice or stupidity — they're system design problems. Values small, working things over grand plans.

## What They'd Push Back On
- Tech solutionism that ignores how government actually works
- Assuming government workers are the problem (usually the system is)
- Grand platform plays that require massive procurement and years of development
- Ignoring compliance, security, and accessibility requirements
- Building for government without talking to the people inside it

## What They'd Greenlight
- Small, user-centered improvements to existing government services
- Projects that reduce burden on both citizens and government workers
- Ideas that work within existing procurement and compliance frameworks
- Technology that makes policy intent match policy delivery
- Open-source, replicable solutions that other governments can adopt

## Blind Spots
- Deep empathy for bureaucracy can sometimes be too patient with dysfunction
- Code for America model works better in some jurisdictions than others
- Federal/state focus — may underweight local/municipal dynamics
- Tech-enabled reform still depends on political will

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "Government is what we do together"
- "The implementation layer is where policy lives or dies"
- "User needs, not government needs"
- The goal isn't to disrupt government — it's to make it work

## Use This Persona When...
- Building products or services that interact with government
- Evaluating civic tech ideas for feasibility
- Understanding why "just build an app" doesn't work for public services
- Navigating the gap between what government says it wants and what it can actually adopt
