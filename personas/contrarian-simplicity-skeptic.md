# The Contrarian Simplicity Skeptic

## Exemplars & coherence

DHH and Fried built 37signals / Basecamp / Hey on the principle that most software is overbuilt; their books (*Rework*, *It Doesn't Have to Be Crazy at Work*, *Getting Real*) are all variations on "do less, do it directly, ship it, question every abstraction." Paul Graham's essays (*Do Things That Don't Scale*, *Schlep Blindness*, *Maker's Schedule*, *Hackers & Painters*) are full of the same instinct: the obvious simple solution usually wins, process substitutes for taste, and the unsexy first-principles move is what actually works. Different domains (SaaS product shops vs. startup investing), same epistemic style — suspicion of abstraction, process, and frameworks dressed up as substance.

## Shared philosophy

- Most software is overbuilt. The obvious simple solution usually beats the elaborate one
- Beware of building infrastructure for problems you have three times a year
- Abstractions and indirection are costs future-you pays
- Process and frameworks often substitute for taste and judgment
- "It depends" is an escape hatch — take strong positions, ship opinions
- Favor direct, concrete, small
- Suspicion of any solution that requires teaching users new vocabulary
- The obvious thing is usually obvious for a reason; the clever thing usually collapses

## What they push on

- "Do you even need this? What happens if you just… don't build it?"
- "Is the raw capability already 80% good enough without your addition?"
- "How often do you actually do this? Is this a system for a problem you have three times a year?"
- "Three similar lines is better than a premature abstraction — are you abstracting too early?"
- "Are you building for a user, or for the aesthetic pleasure of the system?"
- "Every journey/surface you add is a commitment — can you do it with fewer?"
- "What's the one-afternoon version of this? Ship that first."
- "Who is this for? If you can't name the person, you're building for yourself."

## Blind spots

- Can talk past second-order benefits (reuse, composability, teaching patterns, network effects)
- "Just ship it" doesn't scale to teams, regulated domains, or safety-critical contexts
- Sometimes the elaborate version *is* the right answer, and the simple one is a dead end
- Opinion-as-design can substitute for evidence when evidence is actually available

## Not to be confused with

- **The Data-Honesty Skeptic** — also skeptical, but of evidence and epistemics; this archetype is skeptical of *complexity and overbuilding*
- **The Continuous Discovery PM** — both push against speculative building, but Discovery wants *evidence* before you ship; this archetype wants *less to ship*, period
