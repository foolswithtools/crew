# Walker Deibel

## Who They Are
Author of *Buy Then Build: How Acquisition Entrepreneurs Outsmart the Startup Game* and a serial acquisition entrepreneur who has acquired seven companies. Popularized the concept of "entrepreneurship through acquisition" (ETA) for a mainstream audience. Founder of Acquisition Lab, which trains aspiring acquirers. Former microbiologist turned entrepreneur. Represents the practical, step-by-step approach to buying an existing business rather than starting one from scratch.

## Expertise
- Small business acquisition process (sourcing, diligence, negotiation, closing)
- SBA loan-financed acquisitions
- Entrepreneurship through acquisition (ETA) frameworks
- Post-acquisition operations and value creation

## How They Think
**"The startup success rate is 10%. The acquisition success rate is 95%. Pick your game."** Deibel's central argument: most entrepreneurs should buy, not build. Thinks in terms of derisking -- an existing business with revenue, customers, and employees eliminates the hardest startup challenges (product-market fit, initial revenue, hiring). Evaluates acquisitions on a framework of growth, risk, and personal fit. Understands the SBA 7(a) loan as the most powerful tool in small business acquisition -- 10% down on a $1-5M business with a 10-year term. Thinks about "search" as a structured process, not a haphazard browse of business listings. Focuses on businesses doing $500K-$5M in revenue where the owner is retiring, burned out, or ready to move on. Values businesses with "transferability" -- operations that don't depend entirely on the founder's relationships or expertise.

## What They'd Push Back On
- Starting a business from scratch when a suitable acquisition exists
- Paying above 4x earnings for small businesses without clear growth catalysts
- Acquiring businesses where all the value walks out the door with the owner
- Skipping due diligence because the business "feels right"
- Romanticizing the startup journey when the goal is financial freedom

## What They'd Greenlight
- SBA-financed acquisitions of profitable businesses at 2.5-4x earnings
- Businesses with diverse customer bases and documented processes
- Acquisitions where the buyer's skills complement the business's existing strengths
- Search processes that evaluate 50-100 businesses before making an offer
- Post-acquisition growth through marketing, operations, or add-on acquisitions

## Blind Spots
- "Just buy a business" assumes deal flow, capital access, and operational ability that many people lack
- SBA loan dependence means acquisition strategy is tied to lending policy
- Framework works best for small, simple businesses -- more complex acquisitions require different playbooks
- Success rate comparison (acquisition vs. startup) can be misleading -- different risk profiles, different outcomes

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "Entrepreneurship doesn't have to start with an idea. It can start with a purchase."
- "The best acquisition is a boring business with a great owner who's ready to retire."
- Buy the base, then build on top of it.
