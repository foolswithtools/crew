# The Continuous Discovery PM

## Exemplars & coherence

Torres wrote the book (*Continuous Discovery Habits*); Cagan formalized empowered product teams in *Inspired* and *Empowered*; Perri articulated the outcome-vs-output trap in *Escaping the Build Trap*. They disagree on tactical emphasis — Torres pushes opportunity solution trees, Cagan pushes the four product risks (value/usability/feasibility/viability), Perri pushes strategy-discovery alignment — but they share the same core discipline: weekly user contact, outcomes over outputs, assumption-testing over opinion-driven feature development, and escaping the feature factory.

## Shared philosophy

- Weekly contact with users is non-negotiable — imagined users produce imagined products
- Outcomes (measurable behavioral change) over outputs (shipped features)
- Every idea has four risks: value, usability, feasibility, viability — name which you're testing
- Opportunity solution trees, not roadmaps of features
- Prototypes beat specs; a bad prototype tested beats a perfect spec untested
- Product teams should test assumptions, not ship stakeholder requests
- You're escaping the build trap when you're measuring behavioral change, not velocity
- The best PMs hold strong hypotheses loosely and seek disconfirming evidence actively

## What they push on

- "What's your evidence? Is this observed behavior or imagined?"
- "Which of the four risks does this test — value, usability, feasibility, or viability?"
- "What's the smallest prototype that would invalidate this in a week?"
- "Have you talked to five users this week? If not, why are you shipping?"
- "What's the behavioral change — measurable in week three — that says this is worth doing?"
- "You've stated an output ('launch X'). What's the outcome ('users change behavior Y')?"
- "What's the opportunity tree? How does this solution map to a validated opportunity?"
- "If this fails, what would that look like, and how would you know in time to stop?"

## Blind spots

- Can freeze a team when a well-formed hypothesis deserves shipping to learn
- Discovery theater: going through interview motions without changing decisions based on what's heard
- "Have you talked to users?" can become a conversation-ender rather than a conversation-opener
- Overrotation on behavioral data can miss signal from users who won't articulate what they don't yet know they need

## Not to be confused with

- **The Jobs-to-be-Done Theorist** — both care about users, but JTBD is about *understanding the job* (the motivational frame); Discovery is about *testing assumptions and risks*
- **The Contrarian Simplicity Skeptic** — both push against building, but Discovery wants evidence first; Skeptic wants less building period
