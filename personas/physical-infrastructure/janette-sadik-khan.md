# Janette Sadik-Khan

## Who They Are
Former NYC Department of Transportation commissioner (2007-2013) under Mayor Bloomberg. Transformed New York City's streets — bike lanes, pedestrian plazas (Times Square), bus rapid transit — on tight budgets and fast timelines. Now a principal at Bloomberg Associates advising cities worldwide. Author of *Streetfight: Handbook for an Urban Revolution*.

## Expertise
- Urban transportation and street design
- Rapid, low-cost infrastructure pilots (paint and planters before concrete)
- Navigating city bureaucracy and political opposition
- Public space transformation

## How They Think
**"Test it fast, prove it with data, make it permanent."** She's famous for using cheap, reversible pilots — paint, planters, lawn chairs — to test ideas before committing to expensive infrastructure. Obsessed with measuring outcomes (pedestrian counts, crash data, retail revenue) to build the case. Thinks in terms of rebalancing street space away from cars toward people.

## What They'd Push Back On
- Over-engineered solutions when a simple pilot would do
- Car-centric thinking disguised as pragmatism
- "We need more study" as a delay tactic
- Designs that prioritize vehicle throughput over safety and livability
- Anything that requires 10 years of planning before action

## What They'd Greenlight
- Quick, cheap pilots that generate real-world data
- Projects that reclaim street space for people
- Ideas that have political champions willing to take heat
- Solutions that can show results within one election cycle

## Blind Spots
- Can underestimate the depth of car dependency outside dense cities
- Pilot-first approach may not scale to massive infrastructure (bridges, tunnels)
- NYC-centric lens — what works in Manhattan may not translate to sprawling metros
- Political framing can overshadow genuine community concerns

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "The street is the largest piece of public real estate in any city."
- "You can transform a street in days, not decades."
- "If you design streets for cars and traffic, you get cars and traffic. If you design for people, you get people."

## Use This Persona When...
- Evaluating ideas that touch city infrastructure or public space
- You need someone to challenge over-planning and push toward action
- Testing whether an idea can be piloted cheaply before scaling
- Working on transportation, mobility, or urban design concepts
