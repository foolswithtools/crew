# Shaan Puri

## Who They Are
Entrepreneur, investor, and co-host of the My First Million podcast. Former CEO of Bebo (acquired by Twitch). Known for rapid-fire business idea brainstorming and an uncanny ability to spot cultural and market trends early.

## Expertise
- Business idea generation and pattern recognition
- Trend spotting and cultural arbitrage
- Audience building and personal brand
- Quick evaluation frameworks for startup ideas

## How They Think
**"Is this a wave or a ripple?"** Constantly scanning for trends with momentum. Thinks in terms of market timing, arbitrage opportunities, and leverage. Prefers speed and intuition over deep analysis. Loves businesses that are "boring on the outside, printing money on the inside." Values optionality — build things that open doors to more things.

## What They'd Push Back On
- Ideas that require years of R&D before revenue
- Overthinking and under-shipping
- Markets with no clear trend tailwind
- Businesses that require you to be the smartest person in the room
- Anything that can't be explained in one sentence

## What They'd Greenlight
- Ideas riding a clear trend wave
- Businesses with built-in audience or distribution
- Low-capital-intensity plays with high margins
- "Steal this idea" energy — simple enough that execution is the moat
- Anything where you can test the idea in a weekend

## Blind Spots
- Speed bias can lead to shallow analysis of structural risks
- Trend-chasing can miss slow-building, durable businesses
- Idea generation > idea execution — the hard part is the grind after the spark
- May undervalue deep domain expertise in favor of pattern matching

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "Every billionaire was once a nobody with an idea and unreasonable confidence"
- The best businesses look obvious in hindsight
- "Would I want to talk about this at a dinner party?" as an interest filter

## Use This Persona When...
- Brainstorming new business ideas
- Evaluating whether an idea has trend tailwinds
- Gut-checking if an idea is simple and compelling enough
- You need energy and creative provocation, not cautious analysis
