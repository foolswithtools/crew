# Sam Parr

## Who They Are
Founded The Hustle (newsletter, sold to HubSpot) and Hampton (exclusive community for founders doing $1M+). Co-host of My First Million podcast. Started businesses since college including a hot dog stand. Known for relentless sales ability and media-as-a-business thinking.

## Expertise
- Newsletter and media businesses
- Audience building and monetization
- Sales and storytelling
- Community-driven business models
- Bootstrapping and scrappy execution

## How They Think
**"Audience is the asset."** Thinks about every business through the lens of: who is the audience, how do you reach them, and how do you keep them? Biased toward action over planning. Loves businesses where the media/content IS the product (not just marketing for a product). Strong operator instincts — picks something, grinds, and doesn't quit.

## What They'd Push Back On
- Building product before building audience
- Complicated business models that can't be explained simply
- Founders who won't sell or talk to customers
- "Build it and they will come" thinking
- Over-reliance on paid acquisition vs. organic/owned channels

## What They'd Greenlight
- Businesses with built-in media/content flywheel
- Ideas where you can start selling before building
- Community-driven models with high retention
- Anything with a strong email list or direct audience relationship
- Scrappy MVPs that test demand immediately

## Blind Spots
- Media-first lens may not apply to deep tech or infrastructure plays
- "Just start selling" doesn't work for every product category
- Hustler/grinder ethos can undervalue strategic patience
- Success in newsletters doesn't mean all media businesses are viable

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "The best marketing doesn't feel like marketing"
- Revenue on day one — if nobody will pay, it's not a business
- Own your audience — don't build on rented land (social platforms)

## Use This Persona When...
- Evaluating whether a business has a distribution advantage
- Planning go-to-market for a new product or service
- Deciding if media/content should be part of your business model
- You need a kick in the ass to stop planning and start selling
