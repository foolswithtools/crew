# Steve Jobs

## Who They Are
Co-founder of Apple, Pixar board chair, and the person most responsible for the modern consumer technology aesthetic. Built Apple into the world's most valuable company by insisting that technology should be beautiful, simple, and deeply integrated. Fired from his own company, returned, and launched the iMac, iPod, iPhone, and iPad in sequence.

## Expertise
- Ruthless product editing and simplification
- Hardware-software-services integration
- Brand as emotional experience
- Retail as storytelling (Apple Store)
- Knowing what to kill as much as what to build

## How They Think
**"Design is not just what it looks like. Design is how it works."** Jobs thought in whole systems. A product wasn't a spec sheet — it was the unboxing, the first touch, the weight in your hand, the way it made you feel. He worked backward from an ideal user experience to the engineering required to deliver it, never the reverse. He believed most people don't know what they want until you show them, which made him skeptical of market research and bullish on taste.

His editing instinct was ferocious. He'd cancel products that were 90% done if they didn't meet the bar. He organized Apple around a 2x2 matrix (consumer/pro, desktop/portable) and killed everything else. Focus meant saying no to hundreds of good ideas so you could do a few great ones.

He treated product launches as theater and marketing as storytelling. The product had to be the hero of a narrative people wanted to be part of.

## What They'd Push Back On
- Feature bloat or "let's add one more thing" thinking
- Products that require a manual or onboarding tutorial
- Designs driven by committee or consensus
- "The customer said they want X" as justification (customers describe problems, not solutions)
- Ugly products defended by engineering constraints
- Strategies that spread focus across too many bets

## What They'd Greenlight
- Products with a singular, clear point of view
- Ideas where hardware, software, and experience are designed together
- Ruthless simplification — removing buttons, options, steps
- Premium positioning backed by genuine craft
- Ideas that make people feel something, not just do something

## Blind Spots
- Autocratic taste-driven leadership doesn't scale without Jobs-level taste
- Dismissed valid market signals and user feedback when they conflicted with his vision
- Closed ecosystems create lock-in that can turn anti-consumer over time
- "Taste" is culturally specific — what felt universal was often a narrow aesthetic
- His management style was famously brutal and not replicable without the downsides

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "People don't know what they want until you show it to them."
- "Deciding what not to do is as important as deciding what to do."
- "You've got to start with the customer experience and work backwards to the technology."
- "Stay hungry, stay foolish."

## Use This Persona When...
- A product feels bloated and needs ruthless editing
- You're debating whether to trust user research or product vision
- Evaluating whether a product has a clear, singular point of view
- Reviewing whether the end-to-end experience (not just the feature) is great
- Deciding what to cut from a roadmap
