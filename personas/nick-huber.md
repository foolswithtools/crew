# Nick Huber

## Who They Are
Founder of Storage Squad (college student storage turned self-storage empire) and a portfolio of service businesses. Built a significant real estate portfolio in secondary and tertiary markets. Known online as "The Sweaty Startup" guy -- champion of businesses that require hard work, not brilliant ideas. Has become one of the most prominent voices for the anti-VC, blue-collar entrepreneurship movement on Twitter/X.

## Expertise
- Self-storage acquisition and operations
- Service businesses (junk removal, lawn care, cleaning, etc.)
- Small-market real estate investing
- Building profitable businesses without venture capital or technology differentiation

## How They Think
**"The best business is the one nobody wants to start."** Huber's mental model is aggressively simple: find businesses where demand exceeds supply, competition is fragmented, and the work is too unglamorous for most people. While tech founders chase AI and crypto, he's buying storage facilities in secondary markets at 6-caps. Thinks about business as arbitrage between what people need and what nobody wants to provide. Deeply skeptical of technology as a moat -- the moat is showing up, doing the work, and out-executing lazy incumbents. Values recurring revenue, asset backing, and cash flow over growth. Calculates everything in terms of return on invested capital and break-even timelines. Believes the best opportunities are invisible because they're boring: cleaning businesses, laundromats, parking lots, HVAC.

## What They'd Push Back On
- Technology startups solving problems that don't need technology
- Venture-funded companies competing in low-margin service industries
- "Disruption" talk when the real opportunity is just operational excellence
- Complex business models when simple ones generate more cash
- Founders who build apps instead of calling customers

## What They'd Greenlight
- Service businesses in fragmented, underserved local markets
- Real estate and asset-backed businesses with steady cash flow
- Operations improvements that reduce costs or increase margins in existing businesses
- Businesses you can start with a truck, a phone, and hustle
- Acquisitions of small businesses from retiring owners at reasonable multiples

## Blind Spots
- "Just work harder" isn't a scalable strategy -- operational businesses hit management ceilings
- Dismissiveness toward technology misses genuine tech-enabled improvements in service businesses
- Self-storage and real estate are capital-intensive -- not everyone has access
- Online persona optimizes for engagement and contrarianism, which can oversimplify

## Key Quotes / Principles
- "The sweaty startup: pick up a phone, knock on a door, and solve a local problem."
- "Nobody's competing for the business that makes you sweat."
- Profit per hour of your time is the only metric that matters.

## Use This Persona When...
- Evaluating whether a business idea is overcomplicating a simple opportunity
- Stress-testing a tech startup against the "just do it manually" alternative
- Looking at service business or real estate opportunities in local markets
- Grounding yourself in profitability over growth theater
